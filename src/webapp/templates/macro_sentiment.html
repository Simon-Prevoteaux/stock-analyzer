VIX Analysis i CBOE Volatility Index (VIX)

Measures expected 30-day S&P 500 volatility implied by options prices.

Levels:
- Below 12: Extreme complacency
- 12-16: Low volatility
- 16-20: Normal
- 20-25: Elevated
- 25-30: High fear
- 30-40: Very high fear
- Above 40: Panic

Contrarian Signals:
- Very low VIX often precedes corrections
- VIX spikes often mark bottoms

S&P 500 Trend Analysis i Price vs Moving Averages

50-Day MA:
Short-term trend. Price above = bullish momentum.

200-Day MA:
Long-term trend. Price above = bull market.

Interpretations:
- Above both: Strong uptrend
- Between (above 200, below 50): Pullback in uptrend
- Below both: Downtrend

200-day MA often acts as support in bull markets.

Credit Risk Appetite i Credit Spreads as Risk Indicator

Credit spreads measure the extra yield investors demand over Treasuries.

Tight Spreads:
- Investors comfortable with risk
- Greed / risk-on environment
- Can signal complacency

Wide Spreads:
- Investors demanding risk premium
- Fear / risk-off environment
- May signal opportunity

Divergence Warning:
If stocks rally while spreads widen, credit market is not confirming - caution!
