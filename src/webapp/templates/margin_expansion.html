What is Margin Expansion?

Margin expansion occurs when a company's profit margins improve over time while maintaining revenue growth. This indicates the business is becoming more efficient and profitable as it scales.

Key Metrics:

  • Operating Leverage: Earnings Growth √∑ Revenue Growth. Values > 1.0 mean earnings are growing faster than revenue (improving profitability).
  • Margin Trend: Compares recent 4 quarters vs previous 4 quarters. "Expanding" means margins are improving.
Operating Leverage > 1.5

Exceptional efficiency gains - earnings growing much faster than revenue

Operating Leverage 1.0-1.5

Good efficiency - margins expanding alongside growth

Operating Leverage < 1.0

Margins not improving - revenue growing faster than earnings

Default: 15% (moderate growth)
